### Key Trade-Offs

- **Bunker OS wins when:** You want light, fast, forgetful isolation on modern hardware (laptops/tablets/phones). Reboot is absolution. No rot. No bloat. No fear of experiments.  
- **Qubes wins when:** You need extreme isolation (VM escapes are rare). Legacy apps (Windows VMs). Mature tools.  
- **FreeBSD wins when:** You want server/NAS reliability, ZFS, ports. Not for mobile.  
- **NixOS wins when:** You want declarative reproducibility. Not for isolation-first.  
- **Traditional Linux wins when:** You want easy, familiar, broad hardware. But it rots.  
- **Silverblue wins when:** You want immutable Fedora desktop. But less isolation.  
- **GrapheneOS wins when:** You want secure mobile. But Android-based, less flexible.

### Verdict

Bunker OS isn't "better" than everything — it's different. It wins for people who hate rot, want instant resets, and need secure multi-context on low-end hardware. It's lighter than Qubes, simpler than NixOS, more forgetful than FreeBSD, and more flexible than GrapheneOS. If you're building for "normal people who want safe without thinking," Bunker is the sweet spot. If you're paranoid, Qubes. If you're a sysadmin, FreeBSD.
